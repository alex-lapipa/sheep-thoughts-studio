## The Sugarloaf Mountain Profile

The Sugarloaf (Irish: Ó Cualann) is the defining silhouette of the Wicklow landscape.

### Key Visual Characteristics

| Feature | Description |
|---------|-------------|
| **Shape** | Distinctive conical peak with a sharp, slightly asymmetric summit |
| **Profile** | Clean triangular silhouette rising abruptly from rolling hills |
| **Summit** | Often has a darker, rockier cap with exposed quartzite |
| **Slopes** | Gradient from dark purple-brown at peak → heather mauve → green base |
| **Base** | Merges into undulating farmland and forestry |

### Color Observations from Reference Photos

**Mountain Colors (top to bottom)**
- Summit: `#4A4453` — Dark granite/quartzite, purple-grey
- Upper slopes: `#6B5B6B` — Heather-covered, mauve-brown
- Mid slopes: `#7A8B5A` — Mixed heather and grass, muted green-brown
- Lower slopes: `#5D7A3D` — Forestry green, conifer dark

**Sky Conditions**
- Overcast: `#C4C8CC` — Soft grey, diffused light
- Clear day: `#87CEEB` to `#A8D8EA` — Pale blue, Irish soft light
- Golden hour: `#E8D5B8` — Warm cream haze at horizon

**Foreground Fields**
- Lush pasture: `#4A7C34` — Rich grass green
- Hay fields: `#C9B458` — Golden-yellow stubble
- Boggy areas: `#8B7355` — Peat brown with russet tones

## Layered Landscape Composition

The Wicklow landscape creates natural parallax depth through distinct layers:

### Layer 1: Foreground (0-200m)
- Bright green pastures with field boundaries
- Sheep grazing (white dots against green)
- Stone walls and hedgerows
- Rushes and bog cotton in wet areas

### Layer 2: Mid-ground (200m-1km)
- Mixed deciduous and conifer woodland
- Dark green forest blocks creating texture
- Occasional white farmhouses
- Gorse patches (golden-yellow dots)

### Layer 3: Background Hills (1-3km)
- Rolling drumlins and low hills
- Patchwork of fields in varying greens/browns
- Softer, more muted colors due to atmospheric haze
- Purple-tinged heather on higher ground

### Layer 4: Sugarloaf (3km+)
- Dominant conical silhouette
- Color shifts to blue-grey with distance
- Often has cloud shadow or mist effects
- Sharp peak contrasts with softer surroundings

## Atmospheric Conditions

### Typical Irish Light
- Soft, diffused (overcast is common)
- No harsh shadows
- Colors appear more saturated when wet
- Mist sits in valleys creating depth

### Weather Variations for Hero Banners
| Condition | Sky | Mountain | Foreground |
|-----------|-----|----------|------------|
| **Overcast** | Grey gradient | Purple-grey, sharp edges | Rich saturated greens |
| **Sunny** | Pale blue, white clouds | Warm brown-purple | Bright grass, strong shadows |
| **Misty** | White-grey fade | Silhouette only | Lost in haze |
| **Rainy** | Dark grey | Dark, moody | Glistening wet greens |
| **Golden hour** | Warm cream | Glowing edges | Long shadows, warm tones |

## Flora Reference

### Heather (Calluna vulgaris)
- Purple-pink flowers August-September
- Brown-rust color rest of year
- Covers upper mountain slopes

### Gorse (Ulex europaeus)
- Brilliant yellow flowers
- Coconut scent in spring
- Spiky dark green foliage
- Scattered on hillsides

### Bracken (Pteridium aquilinum)
- Green in summer
- Golden-brown in autumn
- Russet-orange when dead
- Creates texture patterns

### Bog Cotton (Eriophorum)
- White fluffy heads
- Grows in wet boggy areas
- Distinctive Irish bog marker

## Implementation Notes for SVG Hero Banners

When translating to our illustrated style:

1. **Simplify the Sugarloaf** to a clean conical silhouette
2. **Use 3-4 distinct layers** for parallax depth
3. **Apply our Wicklow palette** from brand colors
4. **Add atmospheric mist** between layers
5. **Include small details**: sheep, gorse patches, stone walls
6. **Weather effects**: rain lines, cloud movement, sun rays

### SVG Layer Structure
```
Layer 1: Sky gradient (static or animated clouds)
Layer 2: Sugarloaf mountain silhouette
Layer 3: Rolling hills with heather texture
Layer 4: Mid-ground forest/fields
Layer 5: Foreground bog with details
Layer 6: Mist overlay (animated opacity)
```
