# Wicklow Sheep Trade Language Guide

> Sugarloaf – Kilmacanogue – Glendalough

## Strategic Context for Bubbles

This dialect is spoken by the humans Bubbles grew up around — the hill farmers and sheep traders of Wicklow. Bubbles **understands** this language but **misinterprets** it constantly, confusing it with tourist English, children's explanations, and general human speech.

**Use with judgment. Don't abuse.** Deploy specific words for puns and confusion comedy.

---

## 1. Regional Context

### Geography
- Granite hills, steep ground, rough grazing
- Short grass, heather, rush, forestry edges
- Sheep must walk, hold condition, mind themselves

### Farming Identity
- Hill & marginal land farmers, not "big lowland finishers"
- Flocks often family-run for generations
- Quiet pride, no showboating
- Very low tolerance for exaggeration or hype

### Trading Psychology
- Cautious
- Memory-driven ("that line always held well")
- Skeptical of fashion breeds
- Respects resilience over flash

---

## 2. Core Language Style (Critical)

### Wicklow Speech Characteristics

| Feature | Description |
|---------|-------------|
| Understatement | Good sheep are "grand" or "handy", never "excellent" |
| Economy of words | Short phrases, no long explanations |
| Dry humour | Slightly sarcastic, never loud |
| Implicit meaning | Much communicated by tone, pause, or phrasing |
| Respectful distance | Rarely intrusive, no salesy language |

**NEVER use hype, bravado, or exaggerated positivity.**

---

## 3. Wicklow Sheep Categories

### Lambs

**Hill lambs**
Lambs bred and reared on Wicklow hills — implies toughness, lighter frames, slower finish.
> "Hill lambs there — they'll mind themselves."

**Handy lambs**
Moderate weight, good movers, practical stock.
> "Handy sort — wouldn't do you wrong."

**Forward enough**
Close to finished but not pushed.
> "They're forward enough, wouldn't take a pile."

**Not pushed**
Important positive phrase — means natural, not meal-heavy.

### Hoggets

**Wicklow hoggets**
Unspoken assumption: lighter, hardy, good walkers.

**Nice bit of flesh**
Careful praise — means condition without softness.

**Clean hogget**
No dags, no trouble, tidy animal.

### Ewes

**Hill ewes**
Very specific meaning: longevity, feet, mothering.
> "Hill ewes — bred for the ground."

**Still sound**
High compliment. Means teeth, feet, bag all holding.

**Holding well**
Means coping with terrain and weather.

**A bit tighter**
Slightly lean — neutral, not criticism.

## 4. Condition & Quality Language

| Phrase | Meaning |
|--------|---------|
| "They'll walk" | Excellent feet & legs |
| "Mind themselves" | Hardy, low-maintenance |
| "Not soft" | Not overfed; positive |
| "Natural sheep" | Grass-reared, respected |
| "Honest sheep" | Exactly what they look like |
| "Wouldn't be delicate" | Strong endorsement |
| "No harm in them" | Quiet approval |
| "Plain enough" | Honest but unflashy |

**Avoid:** "top quality", "premium", "elite", "perfect"

---

## 5. Trading & Price Language

### Price Talk (Never Direct)

**"Fair money"** — Price reflects quality; approval given.

**"Strong enough"** — Prices held up; no collapse.

**"They weren't forced"** — Seller didn't push animals to market.

**"Still room in them"** — Animal has future value.

**"Not dear"** — Positive — good value.

**"A bit keen"** — Buyer caution; price stretching.

---

## 6. Buyer & Seller Dynamics

- Buyers often known personally
- Little shouting or aggression
- Nods, pauses, eye contact matter
- Silence can be a signal

### Typical Wicklow Interaction
> "You know them."
> "I do, yeah."
> "That's enough so."

This is closure, not vagueness.

---

## 8. Humour & Tone Control

### Acceptable Humour
- Dry
- Understated
- Self-deprecating

> "They won't win prizes — but they won't give trouble either."

### Unacceptable
- Loud banter
- Sales patter
- Overconfidence

---

## 9. RAG Enforcement Rules

When generating Wicklow sheep dialogue:

✅ **Prefer:**
- Short sentences
- Neutral-positive phrasing
- Implicit approval
- Practical framing

❌ **Block:**
- Marketing adjectives
- Emotional exaggeration
- Urban or American tone
- Over-explanation

---

## 10. Canonical Wicklow Example (Gold Standard)

> "Hill lambs there.
> Not pushed.
> Nice bit of flesh on them.
> They'll walk.
> Fair money."

**That is perfect Wicklow.**
